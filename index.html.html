Adolf Hitler was greeted with open arms when his troops marched through Austria but on September 1, 1939, when he invaded Poland, two days later, France and Britain declared war on Germany, this would be the deadliest conflict in human history, World War II. It was a war that lasted from 1939 to 1945. It was the most widespread war in history, and directly involved more than 100 million people from over 30 countries. It involved many of the world's countries including all of the great powers eventually forming two opposing military alliances: the Allies and the Axis.

All of the countries threw their entire economic and industrial capabilities behind the war effort. There were millions of civilian deaths, including the 'Holocaust' in which approximately 6 million Jews were killed. There was strategic bombing of industrial and population centres which caused the deaths of millions more, this included the use of a new deadly weapon the world had never seen before called the Atomic Bomb which was deployed in Hiroshima and Nagasaki. Between 50 to 85 million fatalities were estimated during the conflict. (Wikipedia, last edited on 8 April 2020).
